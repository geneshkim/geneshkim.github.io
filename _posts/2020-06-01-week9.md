Week 9: This past weekend, the NFB asked me to help out with some programming last-minute. I helped develop a college readiness workshop for blind students and was suddenly shipped off to Mississippi. It was another great experience and broadened my perspective on what an everyday "user" means. The students I worked with came from completely different bckgrounds from myself and many blind friends that I have, which just goes to show that disability (even the same condition) is very much a spectrum and intersection of experiences. That being said, I do believe that a single tool can be made useful to a diverse population; it's just a matter of figuring out how to design the tool effectively.
I spent this week reflecting on how the pilot study went. It went well and I am excited to continue running users. I was unfortunately on the road for the next few weeks, but I made a plan to continue recruiting people after my trip.
