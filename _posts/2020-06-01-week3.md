After sending Faraz some sample models to test caption, I flew to Houston to attend the 2023 National Federation of the Blind's national convention. While being a researcher is an important part of my identity, being a blind advocate and community-builder for other blind people is another part of my life that I find most meaningful. I also think it's incredibly valuable to have deep personal connections to a community if you are claiming to be doing research that benefits them. I've been a member and leader in the NFB since I discovered them in 2019, and reconnecting with old friends and mentors was an incredibly refreshing reminder of why I do research and what kind of impact I want to make both in and out of it.
Dan Fan, a lab mate and mentor at Stanford, also attended convention. We ran a data science education accessibility workshop with an EdTech prototype, and also had the chance to present our past work on accessible data visualization at convention. This was a busy time, but a very fun and valuable one.
