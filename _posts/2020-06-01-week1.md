I couldn't be more excited about my first time in Boston. After a few mishaps with my housing on Friday, June 16, I settled in and began thinking more about my summer project on making 3D modeling more accessible for blind folks. I had the opportunity to reconnect with Doctor Hofmann after first meeting her in my 2022 REU experience at the University of Washington. I was incredibly grateful that she was investing so much direct time in her students because I had mostly worked with grad students in the past labs I've worked for. We talked about making the most out of the summer, set an ambitious goal to submit to CHI at the end of my summer, and explored different sub-projects within the space of accessible 3D modeling. I realized that I wanted to gain more experience developing a system and writing a systems-paper rather than other HCI work. I spent the week thinking of the features, limitations, and technologies I would need to build a system, and what system I was trying to build exactly.
